I have been a writer and editor for a while.

My training as a journalist began 6 years ago, in my first semester of my undergrad education in Seattle. My first subject was the abandoned fountain sculpture located in the heart of a community college there. The metal artwork is a part of a complicated political era in the US where Japanese-American citizens were treated like enemies during the World War II. I reported on this topic for years for different local newspapers in the city, even as my reporting grew to include other issues. Read my latest, and most extensive to date, story on this piece of American history here.

Fast forward a year after graduation, I came back to Jakarta and became the Deputy Editor at VICE and worked on English-language content coming out of the Indonesia office. I wrote and edited hundreds of stories, though some of my favorites are under the theme of healthcare, women’s issues, environment, and internet culture. I left the company in May 2019, having served as Coordinating Editor for VICE's Asia offices for months, after deciding I want to pivot into the tech industry. Read all stories I wrote for VICE here.

My latest writing project was commissioned by Indonesia’s Ministry of Marine Affairs and Fisheries. The project serves as a legacy document of former Minister Susi Pudjiastuti’s work from 2014 to 2019. For more than three months, I researched and wrote about her "controversial" work, and the many things she and her team did in five years to reform Indonesia’s fisheries. This document has not been published as of October 2019.
